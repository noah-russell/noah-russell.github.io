Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

I've started making sure my diet actually sustains me and gives me what my body needs. I achieve this by managing the food I buy, making a habit of cooking and preparing meals at appropriate times, and taking vitamins and supplements to ensure a well-rounded diet. I also need to get more physical exercise which I plan to do by training cardio and muscles regularly.

Taha Hinengaro (emotional and mental wellbeing)

Keeping a positive, optimistic attitude, embracing challenges, taking breaks regularly, and taking time to do something creative at least once a week. Socializing and keeping up with family also goes a long way with mental and emotional health.

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

I try to adhere to my values, and I try to choose values that benefit me and the people I care about. I'll continue to explore my values and beliefs in an attempt to better articulate them.

Taha Whānau (family wellbeing)

I make sure to spend time with my family and friends, and keep in touch with them often through social media. I also make things for them as it's my way of showing love.

Whenua (land)

I like to think I notice things about the world that people often overlook or take for granted. I love staring at the night sky, pondering the universe, looking out for shooting stars and satellites. Or even just watching and listening to the birds in the back yard. I believe taking moments to be present in the outside world is important for my mind.
