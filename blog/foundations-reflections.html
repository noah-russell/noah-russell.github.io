What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

First, I learned that I lack a great deal of self-discipline of the mind, and that it's a skill I need to learn rapidly if I'm to keep up with the work load. I learned that while I may be technically capable of achieving a task, I still face challenges that affect the pace and quality of my learning, and I have had to be open and honest with myself if I'm to reach my potential. I also learned that when I don't feel confident about my learning abilities, I tend to lose confidence in general, so I need to stay on top of the learning if I'm to retain a healthy mind.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

I believe my values, my belief in the importance of empathy, and my increased self-awareness dictate my motivations, and therefore my actions. My intention is to improve myself to be the best version of myself that I can be so that I have the most to offer those around me.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

How challenging it would be to actually implement. Even being as open to it as I was, I found it really difficult. I was also surprised by how much trouble I had articulating my thoughts and answers.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

As I just mentioned, implementing the learning. Without a rigid structure/timetable to work from, I had to determine my own, and my values while solid in my own mind, weren't quite aligning with my work output. I had to make very conscious effort to be mindful of my learning and behaviour over the past few weeks. Simply being mindful did start to help though.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

It's clear to me that the core skills are the real focus of the learning, and completion of the technical work is simply evidence of well-integrated core skills. They're fundamental to who you are as a person, which determines what kind of work you'll output, and what kinds of problems you'll be capable of solving.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, in fact I view it as the most valuable learning I'm doing here, for me personally. If I'm able to solidify my core skills, I'll have a solid foundation to build my technical skills on.
