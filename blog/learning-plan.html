Foundations Learning Plan

Ultimately, I'd like to tell stories for a living. The medium could be phyisical, digital, aural, in the form of books or sculptures or songs or paintings or costumes. It could be anything, so long as it tells the story I want to tell at the time. I'd also like to make a comfortable living, and so my plan is to work a career of programming while I build by narrative projects in my spare time. I also fully intend to integrate the skills I learn in programming to my creative work, in as many ways as possible.

I'm a slow learner, but a thorough one. My rate of learning is limited by my lack of ability to sustain focus, and I find it takes me a while to articulate my thoughts clearly. My time-management skills suck as a result, and I tend to spend too long on tasks that I could do much quicker if I only focused properly. These are all skills I know I need to cultivate and nourish with practice if I'm to become someone able and willing to learn and work productively. Once I've conquered the self-discipline of focus, flow-state, and time-management, I'll be capable of realizing my full potential. My strength lies in my passion for problem-solving. I understand the world as a place wherein problems arise continually, and those problems become responsibilities - someone has to take responsibility for solving them, big or small. I believe one has to voluntarily take responsibility for things in life, and that what you choose to be responsible for determines the person you become, whether you're conscious of it or not. I enjoy too any chance I get to incoporate new skills into my creative projects, as I see skills and processes like tools for presenting a particular perspective or narrative to the world. I tend to develop the most expresive skills to the highest degree, the more you can do with a skill, the more reason to develop it. Ideally, I'd like to master focus and time-management, and as much of the technical stuff as I can.

I've been given a specific date that everything needs to be completed by, and I've skimmed through all the Foundations handbook material in advance so I'm aware of what needs to be done, and roughly how long each thing will take to do. Between now and the due date, I've allocated hours and progress to particular days, and as long as I stick to a consistent pace, I'll get everything done in time. I'll take a lot of breaks, aiming to take less as time goes on - try to build up that focus. Mostly I intend to seek help from the internet, MDN is super helpful, and I'm beginning to meet more and more people who program for a living, so I'll begin asking them for help as I start needing it.

From the facilitation team, I expect to learn WHAT we should be focusing on learning, and hopefully HOW to learn. I would really benefit from a wide range of opinions on how to enforce self-discipline.

Other commitments? There's always life stuff, life events and the like. But nothing recurrent
