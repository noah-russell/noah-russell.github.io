Neuroplasticity is a fundamental property of the brain and how it learns, remembers, and adapts to new experiences. It's the brain's ability to build up neural pathways, restructuring itself, in order to learn and adapt is the reason we can remember speech, or how to drive cars, or how to write or spell words or tell the time, etc.! A growth mindset is the idea that your brain and your mind aren't fixed entities. It's the recognition that skills can be gained through exploration and repetition. This is vital when entering a rapid-learning environment, like the tech world. I guess it was instilled by my parents, but I've always understood that I could learn anything I set my mind to, even if it was hard. I have, however, found myself habitually avoiding challenges as of late, simply as a result of bad habits that have formed over the last couple years.
